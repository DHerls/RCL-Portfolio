Multimedia

Over the last few decades, there has been an increase in frequency of mass shootings that correlates with the rise of mass media and more recently, social media. There is some evidence to support that the platform given to the perpetrators of mass shootings in the media can lead to "copycat" shootings. In light of this evidence, some people believe that the media should not report on the shooters themselves so as to not inspire other shootings. Others feel that this would be tantamount to censorship and should be avoided.

The video examines evidence and motivations on both sides of this controversial issue. It looks at the current situation as well as historical parallels in order to provide the viewer with a clearer picture of both sides of the controversy.
